Our Story

How it started

It started at home.

I started baking out of my apartment kitchen, mostly for my sister Louma. Watching her settle into a Saturday morning routine, the timer, the smell of butter, the way she greeted the regulars who started showing up, is when I knew this had to be a real place.

The food

European technique. Levantine flavor.

81-layer croissants laminated by hand, sourdough cold-fermented for 72 hours, alongside zaatar, labneh, pistachio, and orange blossom.

The founder
Milla Ghandour

I trained in Paris and Beirut, then came home to West LA to build a bakery worth my sister's name. Every loaf and every latte here is part of a place I always wanted her to walk into.

Milla

Mission

The Louma Initiative trains and employs people with intellectual and developmental differences as bakers, baristas, and front-of-house teammates. They work the line in plain view, not in a separate program behind a door. Every dollar the bakery makes funds wages, training, and a real path to a career in food.

Each teammate trains alongside our head bakers and baristas. They learn lamination, espresso, customer service, and how to run a station from open to close.

We hire to keep. Full-time hours, real wages, benefits, and a job that gets harder and more interesting over time.
